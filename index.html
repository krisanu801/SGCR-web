Abstract

We study cross-modal alignment between independently pretrained vision (DINOv2) and language (all-MiniLM-L6-v2) encoders using the functional map framework from computational geometry. While the framework reveals that independently trained models develop manifolds of comparable intrinsic complexity (normalized spectral distance 0.043), it also uncovers a "spectral complexity–orientation gap": the eigenvector bases are effectively unaligned. This gap defines a boundary condition for spectral alignment methods and motivates new diagnostic quantities for characterizing cross-modal representation compatibility.

The Functional Map Framework

Functional maps reformulate correspondence between two manifolds as a compact linear operator \(C \in \mathbb{R}^{k \times k}\) between their Laplace–Beltrami spectral bases. Given two manifolds \(\mathcal{M}_1, \mathcal{M}_2\), a pointwise map \(T: \mathcal{M}_1 \to \mathcal{M}_2\) induces a linear operator represented by \(C\) in the truncated spectral basis.

\[ \mathcal{L}_m \phi_{m,j} = \lambda_{m,j} \phi_{m,j}, \quad 0 = \lambda_{m,1} \leq \lambda_{m,2} \leq \dots \leq \lambda_{m,k_s+1} \]

The functional map \(C\) is obtained by solving a regularized least-squares problem:

\[ C^* = \text{argmin}_C \|CA - B\|_2^2 + \lambda_1 \|C\Lambda_v - \Lambda_t C\|_2^2 + \lambda_2 \|C\|_2^2 \]

Spectral Analysis & Diagnostics

Figure 1: Laplacian Eigenvalue Spectra and Functional Map Diagnostics. The spectra (top-left) are remarkably similar, but the functional map matrix (bottom-left) shows poor diagonal dominance, indicating unaligned bases.

We propose three diagnostic quantities to characterize geometric compatibility:

  • Normalized Spectral Distance: Measures similarity in intrinsic complexity.
  • Diagonal Dominance: Indicates shared spectral orientation.
  • Orthogonality Error: Measures how close the correspondence is to an isometry.

Our findings show a Spectral Complexity–Orientation Gap: models converge in how much structure they capture (similar eigenvalues) but not in how they organize it (unaligned eigenvectors).

Experimental Results

Figure 2: Cross-modal retrieval performance (Image to Text). Functional maps (FMap) are compared against Procrustes, CCA, and Relative Representations.

While functional maps provide deep geometric insight, they currently underperform simpler baselines like Procrustes alignment for retrieval. This is primarily because the isometry assumption—that the mapping preserves distances perfectly—does not hold for independently trained encoders.

Figure 3: Effect of spectral truncation on retrieval performance. Increasing the spectral dimension \(k\) generally improves recall but highlights the orientation gap.

Conclusion

This work provides a finer-grained measurement tool for the Platonic Representation Hypothesis. By separating eigenvalue spectra (complexity) from eigenvector correspondence (orientation), we show that while foundation models converge toward shared statistical representations, their internal coordinate systems remain decoupled.
